The Sound of Nature

The Sound of Nature is dedicated to raising awareness on how damaging tree milling is on our Wētā. Due to tree milling our Wētā are loosing their habitats daily and it is up to us to make a change.

Read Kaia the Wētā and learn about her struggles with habitat loss and deforestation.

Tree milling removes essential microhabitats such as large logs, tree hollows, and dense undergrowth. Research has shown that the removal of these features can significantly impact forest-dwelling insects and other wildlife.

The forestry industry significantly impacts the environment by contributing to deforestation, which leads to habitat loss, decreased biodiversity, and disruptions in carbon storage. However, sustainable forestry practices can help mitigate these effects by promoting responsible tree harvesting and reforestation efforts.

Wētā have become icons for invertebrate conservation in New Zealand because many species are threatened or endangered. There are more than 100 species of wētā in New Zealand, 16 of which are at risk.
